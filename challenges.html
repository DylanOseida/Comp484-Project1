Game development has been one of the most fun things I've worked on, but also one of the hardest. I've faced challenges with design, technical skills, and motivation. Each has taught me something new and shaped how I approach making games.

My Main Challenges

  • Design Challenges

    I never really created my own models or textures. I tried Blender and Krita, but I never completed anything. Design takes a lot of time, and I dont usually have the motivation for it. Roblox Studio made design much easier, because I could use simple shapes to build fun levels quickly, and it still felt like my own design.

  • Technical Challenges

    As a beginner, I often wasn't sure which tools to use in engines like Unity or Godot. Unity's C# coding was something I practiced, but I didnt know how to use Godot's system at all. Going at it solo meant I relied heavily on YouTube tutorials.

    Even in Roblox, I run into technical problems. For example, I struggle with events, tweening, and especially with DT when trying to make object movement smooth.

  • Motivation Challenges

    Motivation is my hardest challenge. Every time I hit a big technical wall, I take a break and sometimes I don't come back for months.

    Here's my current motivation meter to get back into game development:

    40%

    And here's my current progress on my Roblox game:

    70%

Lessons Learned

I've learned the most from Roblox Studio and the Udemy class I took. Udemy helped me understand technical details like FPS, collision boxes, and rigidbodies. Roblox taught me about game design the most, more specifically, planning ahead before building, setting goals, and keeping levels consistent.
