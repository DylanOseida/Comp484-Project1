One of the most common “projects” in game development is participating in a game jam. A game jam is an event where developers come together to create a game in a limited amount of time, usually within a week. These events focus on creativity, rapid prototyping, and teamwork.

What is a Game Jam?

A game jam is like a hackathon, but for video games. Participants are given a theme, a time limit, and then challenged to create a game from scratch. The emphasis is not on creating a polished, perfect product, but on experimenting, learning, and finishing something playable.

Some of the most famous jams include the Global Game Jam, Brackeys Game Jam, and GMTK Game Jam. These usually have thousands of participants from all over the world.

Why Join a Game Jam?

  • Learning: You quickly pick up new skills and tools while under pressure.
  • Creativity: The short time frame pushes developers to think outside the box.
  • Teamwork: Many jams encourage working with others, often with strangers online.
  • Networking: Jams are great places to meet other developers and artists.

How a Game Jam Works

Even though every jam is different, they follow a similar structure:

  1. Announcement of a theme (example: "Risk it for the Biscuit" from Brackeys Game Jam 2025.2).
  2. Brainstorming ideas.
  3. Prototyping and development of the game.
  4. Playtesting and last-minute polish.
  5. Submission of the game.

Reflection on Game Jams

Game jams are one of the best ways to gain experience in game development. They emphasize creativity, problem-solving, and teamwork more than technical perfection. Many professional developers recommend them as a way to practice finishing projects — even small, simple ones — and to build confidence as a game maker.
